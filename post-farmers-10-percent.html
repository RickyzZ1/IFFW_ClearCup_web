Why Farmers Often Receive Only Around 10% Of Final Consumer Value

Consumers may pay premium prices for specialty coffee, but farming households often receive only a small share. This is less about one unfair transaction and more about how the system is structured.

~10%

A commonly referenced estimate of farmer share in end-consumer coffee value

Where value is captured

In many markets, value growth happens downstream through branding, roasting, retail channels, and customer-facing storytelling. At origin, producers still carry production risk while receiving a much smaller piece of final spending.

Why this persists

  • Smallholder supply is fragmented, making direct access expensive and complex.
  • Quality language and pricing frameworks are often controlled away from origin.
  • Farmers face weak bargaining power in volatile commodity conditions.
  • Transparency on premium distribution is limited across the chain.

Why this matters beyond economics

Low value share at farm level affects household life directly: delayed healthcare, unstable school spending, and dependence on short-term debt between harvests. Value distribution is therefore both a trade issue and a social reality.

What The ClearCup tracks per lot

  • Farmer payout ratio
  • Minimum guarantee reference
  • Advance payment terms
  • Traceability and quality records

Premium coffee should not only taste better. It should distribute value more fairly.
